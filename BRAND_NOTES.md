# Natural Abundance — Brand & Business Notes
## For future content reference

### Erik DeGunther — Key Facts
- Based on the Hamakua Coast, Big Island, Hawaiʻi
- 3 years managing 8 acres in Papaikou
- Pure KNF certified practitioner
- Board member of the Pure KNF Foundation
- Uses organic practices — no synthetic inputs, no pesticides, no fertilizers

### Design & Farming Philosophy
- **Agroforestry focus** — syntropic agroforestry methods alongside traditional orchard practices
- **Low-maintenance systems** — designs that succeed with minimal ongoing intervention once established
- **Soil care as spiritual practice** — caring for the soil is not just technique, it's a way of honoring the ʻāina
- **Work WITH the land** — reads slope, soil, water, wind before designing anything
- **Korean Natural Farming (KNF)** — builds biologically active soil through natural inputs

### Core Business Conviction
- Sustainable, holistic land practices can be MORE profitable than conventional, extractive methods
- Tending the ʻāina with care and making money for clients are the same goal, not competing ones
- Erik's mission is to develop and hone these systems and implement them at scale
- If the methods can't pay for themselves, they won't scale — they can, and proving that is the point
- Maximize efficiency creatively: stacking functions, reducing inputs, designing self-maintaining systems
- Without profitability, sustainable farming is just an expensive hobby — the economics have to work

### Messaging Guidelines
- Do NOT use "organic conversion" as a service or talking point
- Service #4 is "Korean Natural Farming & Soil Building" (not "organic conversion")
- Never use emojis — the brand should feel professional and grounded, not AI-generated
- Testimonials: always abbreviate to first name + last initial (e.g., "Amanda R." not "Amanda Rose") to protect client privacy
- Use ʻokina correctly in all Hawaiian words: Hawaiʻi, ʻāina, ʻawa, Pepeʻekeo, etc.
